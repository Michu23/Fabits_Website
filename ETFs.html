Advantages of ETFs

1. Low Cost

Since these funds are not actively managed, they have a significantly lesser expense ratio i.e. costs to manage assets and broker commissions, compared to mutual funds. The average total expense ratio of ETF is 0.16% VS the average Mutual Fund expense ratio is 2%. Here’s a quick check on how much you would save (link, active link to pricing page).

2. Consistent Performance:

The performance of ETFs closely reflects the performance of the index, so you can always expect consistent out-performance when picking ETFs as an investment vehicle.

3. Liquidity:

ETFs are traded on an exchange, much like the manner in which stocks are, and the price of ETFs would change through the trading day. Because of this quality, buying and selling is easy, making ETFs more liquid.

3. Liquidity:

ETFs are traded on an exchange, much like the manner in which stocks are, and the price of ETFs would change through the trading day. Because of this quality, buying and selling is easy, making ETFs more liquid.

4. Skip Entry & Exit Loads:

Unlike mutual funds, there are no entry and exit loads when investing in ETFs. We’ll explain. An entry load is a percentage of fee charged to an investor when entering a mutual fund, and an exit load is a similar fee when exiting a mutual fund.

5. No Lock-in Period:

Unlike mutual funds where there is usually a minimum lock-in period, ETFs generally do not have lock-in periods, which means they can be bought or sold at the investor’s convenience without the incurrence of penalties.

6. No Commissions

There are no commissions paid out to intermediaries when investing in ETFs.

ETFs vs Mutual Funds:

The S&P BSE 100 lost 8.4% over the first half of 2022, and 87.5% of active managers underperformed the benchmark over that period. Underperformance rates for other periods were not far off from the H1 2022 numbers, with the 1-, 3- and 5-year periods at 90.9%, 83.9% and 89.1%, respectively. Over the 10-year period, however, the active manager record is significantly better, with the underperformance rate dropping to 67.4%.

Key Take Aways:

Large percentage of actively managed mutual funds have failed to beat the index it tracks. Very few funds have consistently beaten the index over a 5 year period. To round up, finding the mutual fund that beats the index is a lot like shooting in the dark. The key to long term wealth creation lies in investing in assets that outperform consistently, and remains in the top 33% in its sector. Hence, ETFs!
